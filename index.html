The History of Pompeii

Pompeii was a bustling city of the Roman Empire, known for its vibrant culture and trade. The city thrived until the catastrophic eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD.

It was a city full of life, with theaters, baths, and marketplaces where Romans conducted their daily business.

The Eruption

The eruption of Mount Vesuvius buried Pompeii under meters of ash and pumice. Many citizens perished, while the city's remains were preserved for centuries.

Pompeii Today

Archaeologists have excavated Pompeii, revealing houses, frescoes, and streets frozen in time. It is one of the most significant archaeological sites in the world.

Visitors can walk through the ancient city and imagine life as it was two thousand years ago.
